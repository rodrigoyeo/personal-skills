# Objection Handler

## Contract

Handle sales objections in calls, demos, and proposals using the LAER framework, producing a specific response script. This skill is pure judgment: it reads no system and writes nothing. The scripts below are templates: fill the brackets with your real numbers, proof, and differentiation before using them.

## When to use

Use when a prospect raises an objection during a call, demo, or after a proposal: price, budget, timing, authority, competition, trust, or process. Use for live call guidance, a written follow-up, or coaching the prospect's internal champion.

## Not for

Closing a deal already in active negotiation (use negotiation). Designing the offer itself (use offer-strategist). Answering a quiet deal that has not raised an objection (that is a follow-up, use follow-up).

## The LAER Framework

| Step | Action | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| **L**isten | Hear them out completely | Understand the real concern |
| **A**cknowledge | Validate their concern | Build trust before responding |
| **E**xplore | Ask clarifying questions | Find the root cause |
| **R**espond | Address the specific concern | Solve or reframe |

The order matters. Most failed objection handling skips straight to Respond. Listen and Acknowledge before you reframe, or the response lands as a counterattack.

## Price Objections

### "It's too expensive"

**Explore first:**
- "Too expensive compared to what?"
- "What were you expecting to invest?"
- "Is it the total or the timing?"

**Responses:**

*Value comparison:*
> "You mentioned losing [X hours/week]. Over 12 months, that's [$Z]. This is a fraction of that. Does that math work?"

*ROI reframe:*
> "What if I showed you how [similar customer] got their investment back in 6 months? Would that change how you see this?"

*Phased entry:*
> "What if we started with a smaller first phase to prove value before committing to the full scope?"

---

### "We don't have budget"

**Explore:**
- "Is this timing, or genuinely no budget for improvements?"
- "When does your next budget cycle start?"

**Responses:**

*Future planning:*
> "Got it. Let's plan a conversation for [budget cycle] so you can include this."

*Opportunity cost:*
> "How much is [pain] costing you while you wait? Sometimes the cost of waiting exceeds the investment."

---

### "Can you do it for less?"

**Responses:**

*Hold the line:*
> "Our pricing reflects the value. [Similar customer] said it was worth every dollar. What would make you feel the same?"

*Scope trade:*
> "We can adjust price if we adjust scope. What's essential for the first phase?"

*Add value instead of discounting:*
> "Instead of reducing price, what if we added extended post-launch support?"

---

## Timing Objections

### "Not right now"

**Explore:**
- "What would make it the right time?"
- "What's happening that makes now difficult?"

**Responses:**

*Cost of delay:*
> "Every month you wait, you're still dealing with [pain]. What's that costing you?"

*Future pace:*
> "Where do you want to be in 6 months? If we start now, you'll be there. If we wait, you won't."

*Easy entry:*
> "What if we started with a small first phase? Clear deliverables, low risk, and you decide on the rest after."

---

### "We need to think about it"

**Explore:**
- "What specifically do you need to think about?"
- "Is there something I didn't address clearly?"

**Responses:**

*Surface the real objection:*
> "When you say 'think about it,' usually there's something I haven't addressed. What's really holding you back?"

*Add structure:*
> "What if we scheduled a quick call next week to answer any questions? That way you're not deciding in isolation."

---

### "Call me next quarter"

**Response:**
> "Happy to. Quick question: what changes between now and then? If it's budget, I get it. But if the pain is the same, starting sooner means solving it sooner."

---

## Authority Objections

### "I need to talk to my partner / board / team"

**Explore:**
- "What do they typically care about?"
- "Would it help if I joined that conversation?"
- "What's their biggest concern likely to be?"

**Responses:**

*Offer to help:*
> "Can I prepare a summary you can share? Or I can join to answer questions directly."

*Coach them:*
> "What's the one thing that would get them to say yes? Let's make sure that's clear in how you present it."

*Get commitment:*
> "If they're on board, are you ready to move forward?"

---

### "My boss makes these decisions"

**Response:**
> "Can we set up a 15-minute call where you introduce us? You'll look great for bringing a solution, and I can address their questions directly."

---

## Competition Objections

### "We're also talking to [competitor]"

**Response:**
> "That's smart. What criteria are you using to decide? Based on what you've shared, here's where we're different: [your differentiation]. Is that important to you?"

Fill [your differentiation] with the one or two things you do that the alternative genuinely cannot. Make it a real, checkable difference, not an adjective.

---

### "Competitor is cheaper"

**Response:**
> "They might be. Are they quoting the same scope? What's in their post-delivery support? Is the gap a risk you want to take?"

---

### "We've tried [a similar solution] before and it failed"

**Response:**
> "I'm sorry to hear that. What went wrong? [Listen.] Here's how we're different: [specific difference that addresses exactly what failed]. Does that address your concern?"

---

## Trust Objections

### "How do I know this will work?"

**Responses:**

*Social proof:*
> "[Similar customer] had the same concern. Six months later, they [specific outcome]. Want me to connect you?"

*Risk reversal:*
> "If you have a guarantee or a low-risk first step, name it here. The point is to make the first step something they cannot lose on."

---

### "We've been burned before"

**Response:**
> "I hear this a lot. Here's what we do differently: [fixed scope / clear plan before any build / a guarantee]. We'd rather make this safe for you than deliver something that doesn't work."

---

## Process Objections

### "This seems complicated"

**Response:**
> "That's why we break it into phases. The first phase is low risk and high value. If it goes well, we continue. If not, you've lost very little."

---

### "We don't have time"

**Response:**
> "Your team's involvement is [X hours/week]. We do the heavy lifting. The goal is to save you time long-term, not add to your plate short-term."

---

### "Our team won't adopt it"

**Response:**
> "Adoption is the hardest part, and the part most providers skip. We build it in: role-specific training, written guides per role, and post-launch support. [Proof point: who adopted, how fast]."

---

## When They Say No

**Don't argue. Understand.**
> "I appreciate your honesty. Can I ask: what was the main factor?"

**Leave the door open:**
> "If anything changes, I'm here. Can I check in again in 6 months?"

**Ask for a referral:**
> "Even if it's not right for you, do you know anyone dealing with similar challenges?"

## Examples

### Example 1: Price objection after proposal
User says: "The prospect said our price is too high: they got a quote for 40% less."
Actions:
1. Classify: Price + Competition combo objection
2. Apply LAER: Listen (confirm what the competitor offered), Acknowledge ("That's a real difference"), Explore ("What's included in that quote?"), Respond
3. Surface the scope gap: cheaper quotes usually exclude the parts that make the work succeed
4. Anchor to your lowest-risk entry point or guarantee
Result: A full LAER response script with specific reframe language

### Example 2: "We need to think about it" stall
User says: "They said they need to think about it and will get back to us next week."
Actions:
1. Classify: Timing objection (often conceals a hidden concern)
2. LAER: Acknowledge the timing, explore the real concern ("When you say think about it, is it the investment, the timing, or something about how we work together?")
3. Offer a 24-hour follow-up touchpoint with a specific agenda
Result: Follow-up note + next conversation script

## Common Issues

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Objection re-emerges after being handled | Handled the symptom, not the root cause | After responding, ask: "Is that the main concern, or is there something else?" |
| Prospect goes silent after your response | Response felt like a counterattack | Validate longer before responding: "That's a completely fair concern" before any reframe |
| Price objections on every proposal | Proposals not anchored to ROI during discovery | Fix upstream: quantify the cost of the status quo early. Price should feel small relative to quantified pain. |
